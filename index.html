Your Cretan home

Welcome to ESTIA

ESTIA Stone House is a traditional stone home in the peaceful village of Modi, a short drive from Chania and the beaches of the north coast.

Here, Cretan architecture meets modern comfort: old stone, warm wood, olive trees and the quiet rhythm of village life.

The building

From olive mill to family home

The stones of ESTIA carry more than a century of work, abandonment, perseverance and renewal.

Around 1920

A small stone fabrika

The building was created as a local olive mill. A millstone, press and clay jars filled the space, together with the unmistakable scent of freshly worked olives.

For about fifty years

A place of work and village life

Olive oil was produced here for local families. The building was not simply a workshop; it belonged to the everyday rhythm and memory of the area.

Later years

Storage, abandonment and silence

As production methods changed, the mill closed and became a storage building. Time took its toll: it deteriorated, was abandoned and came close to collapse. One of the few elements still standing was its small window — a quiet sign that the story was not over.

Around 2007

The restoration begins

The goal was not to erase the past, but to protect it. Old stones were reused, the original form was respected and changes were kept as limited as possible.

Today

A new life as ESTIA Stone House

The former olive mill now welcomes people as a home. Its function has changed, but its character, stones and connection to the village remain.

The name

Why we called it “ESTIA”

Our father, Stelios, chose the name. It holds our family inside it, but it also carries one of the oldest and warmest ideas in Greek culture.

✦

Our family initials

ESTIA brings together the names Stelios, Evgenia, Ioanna and Aria — the people whose memories and affection shaped the house.

A little Greek magic

In ancient Greek tradition, Hestia was the goddess of the hearth, home and family. The flame at the centre of the house symbolised warmth, protection, continuity and hospitality. That meaning feels perfectly at home here: an old working building, brought back to life as a place where people can gather, rest and feel welcome.

🌿

Who we are

We are a family deeply connected to Modi, its people and its memories. The house is tied to our childhood summers, to our father’s story and to our wish not to let a piece of our family and local history disappear.

More than anyone, our father Stelios stood behind this effort. Through persistence, personal work and love for the building, he gave life again to a place that once seemed lost. For us, ESTIA is more than accommodation — it is a family memory given shape again.
